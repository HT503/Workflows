For Your Reading Pleasure

A hybrid course?

HT503 became a hybrid course in 2011, when it replaced one in-class lecture with a video lecture each week... You should expect to attend to the video lectures differently than a movie. Some find it helps to take notes or quizzes. Recent reseach suggests that viewing the videos in groups (esp. with other students) may help you better retain the materials. And that creating and sharing related materials with the class may help you, too. We recommend you find a friend or five and share a meal before or after, and that you find a way to contribute to learning.

Too Much Info

While the myth of information overload has been debunked, feeling overwhelmed by the amount of materials to learn is something many HT503 students report. Many also report that in the last few weeks of class, the whole course comes together. Be patient. Talk to your discussion group leader or a TA or Dr. Pacini before you drop or despair.

SafeAssign

Though we sometimes spot plagiarism problems on SafeAssign, we use it primarily because we hope you'll take advantage of what it offers: a chance to see how confident you are with the material. One benefit of writing essays is that it requires you to integrate the materials and to teach them (in writing) to others. If you used lots of long quotes or paraphrased frequently, you could probably go over the material again.
